Fonts

FONTS IN EASY EYES
You can use any font (that is free or licensed for web use) with EasyEyes. Just specify it as the font in a condition in your experiment table and upload the font file to your Pavlovia account by dropping it on the EasyEyes/Threshold page. To minimize transmission time at the beginning of your experiment, we recommend using a compressed web font format (WOFF2 is best), but any of the popular font formats will work.



FREE FONTS
Here we offer links to download a few fonts that are available for research use without charge.

The Sloan font was invented by Louise Sloan (1959) and is the USA standard optotype for acuity testing. It is used in the Pelli-Robson chart to measure contrast sensitivity (Pelli, Robson, and Wilkins, 1988), and Pelli and his collaborators used it to measure crowding (Pelli et al., 2016). Pelli produced the computer version of the Sloan font based on the geometric specifications in Sloan's (1959) paper. Download Sloan.woff2

The Pelli font is extremely skinny, and was invented by Pelli for measuring foveal crowding (Pelli et al., 2016). Download Pelli.woff2

The Auckland Optotype are ten simple pictograms — each an enclosed shape comprised of a single stroke — designed to be nameable by children. The symbols have the advantage of being equally legible at the same size, are available in both black-on-white and vanishing versions, and are free-to-use under a Creative commons license. If you find them useful please cite Hamm, Yeoman, Anstice, & Dakin (2018). We recommend using the pictograms corresponding to the digits "0123456789". Download Auckland Optotype.woff2

Interpol Sans, designed by our friend and colleague, Hannes Famira, "Interpol Sans is the sans serif branch of a family of robust, low contrast typefaces designed for legibility in low resolution situations. It performs particularly well on media like television and computer screens, or in projections and on light boxes. The Interpol family of typefaces also runs a bit narrow. While it is not quite condensed, Interpol Sans consumes less space than classic proportions prescribe. In Dutch this is called zuinig: thrifty. It means that the Interpol will fit just a few more words in one line of text than a run-of-the-mill sans serif. This is interesting for narrow columns and headlines, or for publications which need to accommodate a lot of text on a page without looking crammed." (Quoted from here.) Sold by TypeNetwork and Adobe.
We bought a license for any web page served by Pavlovia, e.g., your EasyEyes experiment, to use the regular style Interpol Sans font from the font vendor’s server (up to 10,000 uses per month). Set font to https://cloud.typenetwork.com/projects/6094/fontface.css and fontSource to server.

FONT SOURCES
Google Fonts offers a wide range of open access fonts. MyFonts.com sells over 100,000 fonts (for desktop and web), from most of the world's foundries. For web use in EasyEyes you must have a web font license. The desktop license is irrelevant.
