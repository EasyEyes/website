Fonts

FONTS IN EASY EYES
You can use any font (that is free or licensed for web use) with EasyEyes. Just specify it as the targetFont in a condition in your experiment table and upload the font file to your Pavlovia account by dropping it on the EasyEyes/Threshold page. To minimize transmission time at the beginning of your experiment, we recommend using a compressed web font format (WOFF2 is best), but any of the popular font formats will work.


FREE FONTS
Here we offer links to download a few fonts that are available for research use without charge.

The Sloan font was invented by Louise Sloan (1959) and is the USA standard optotype for acuity testing. It is used in the Pelli-Robson chart to measure contrast sensitivity (Pelli, Robson, and Wilkins, 1988), and Pelli and his collaborators used it to measure crowding (Pelli et al., 2016). Pelli produced the computer version of the Sloan font based on the geometric specifications in Sloan's (1959) paper.
Download Sloan.woff2.

The Pelli font is extremely skinny, and was invented by Pelli for measuring foveal crowding (Pelli et al., 2016).
Download Pelli.woff2.

The Auckland Optotype was invented by Steven Dakin to measure crowding in children.
Download Auckland Optotype.woff2.


FONT SOURCES
Google Fonts offers a wide range of open access fonts. MyFonts.com sells fonts from most of the world's foundries. For web use in EasyEyes you must buy the web font license. The desktop license is irrelevant.
