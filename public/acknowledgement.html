  • Acknowledgement

EasyEyes is open source, and builds on contributions from many colleagues whom we thank here. The credit card trick for measuring screen size and the blind spot detection for measuring viewing distance were both suggested by Li et al. (2020). Offering to use a USB connector instead of a credit card was suggested by Ajay Subramanian. The gaze tracking is based on the open-source WebGazer.js package from Papoutsaki et al. (2016). WebGazer and our head tracking are both built on Google's open-source FaceMesh software (Kartynnik et al., 2019). The EasyEyes icon is a block print by Cassandra Hussey, used with permission. Other dependencies of our software can be found in the package configuration file in the corresponding repositories on GitHub.

As we were wrapping up our development of Remote Calibrator, we realized that Thomas Pronk also made a demonstrative project showing integrating WebGazer into PsychoJS. We would love to acknowledge his work here as well.

Pronk, T. (2020). Demo of Eye-Tracking via Webcam in PsychoJS (Version 2) [Computer software]. Retrieved from https://gitlab.pavlovia.org/tpronk/demo_eye_tracking2/.
